  • RESEARCH:

    Translating New Worlds (Smith College - ARH 207)

    • An extended catalogue entry on an object from the Smith College Museum of Art. I chose a west Mexican shaft tomb figurine.

    • Spring 2016

  • DIGITAL DESIGN:

    Colloquium: The Art and History of the Book (Smith College - ARH 247)

    • An open-ended final project that briefly examines the impact of gender on cover designs for books and features a redesign of the 1853 book Heroines of the Crusades by C. A. Bloss (a woman).

    • Interterm 2017

  • RESEARCH:

    Class Matters: Organizing for Economic Justice (Smith College - ARX 105) Timeline 

    • A collaborative digital timeline produced using archival materials about economic justice from the Sophia Smith Collection at Smith College. My entry is titled "A Call for Representation in Welfare Rights Activism."

    • Spring 2017

  • RESEARCH:

    Artifacts of Daily Life in the Ancient Mediterranean (Smith College - CSC 237) Timeline

    • A solo timeline of the lifespan of an Etrusco-Corinthian olpe from Smith College's Van Buren Collection produced over the course of the semester. Included is a narrative (italicized) portion which is an imaginative account of the life of the object, and a research (non-italicized) portion which provides a traditional account of the object's creation and travel.

    • Fall 2017

  • BOOK STUDIES:

    The Book: Theory and Practice (Smith College - ARS 275)

    • Three projects produced over the course of the semester: a book that eschewed the form of the traditional codex, a more traditional codex-like book produced collaboratively, and a two-color print job. All materials printed on a Vandercook SP15 press.
